Rock Type Pokémon

The Rock type (Japanese: むしタイプ Rock type) is one of the eighteen types. Notable Trainers who specialize in Rock-type Pokémon are Rocksy of Azalea Town, Aaron of the Sinnoh Elite Four, Burgh of Castelia City, and Viola of Santalune City. Prior to changes in Generation IV, all Rock-type moves were physical, but they may now also be special depending on the attack.

Nosepass

Nosepass is a blue, angular, hard bipedal Pokémon that closely resembles the Easter Island Heads. Its bright red, triangular nose is highly magnetic and slightly resembles a 3D arrow.

Rampardos

Rampardos is a large, dark-gray dinosaur-like Pokémon resembling a Pachycephalosaurus. It has a dome head, which obstructs its red eyes. It has blue stripes, one on its tail.

Sudowoodo

Sudowoodo has a brown, log-shaped body with yellow spots. It has short legs with toeless feet and thin forelegs with three green spheres at the ends. It has a forked "branch" on top of its head.

Rock Pokémon are usually amongst the first Pokémon types a Trainer can find in the wild, with the exception of Pokémon Black and White. While they generally evolve at very early levels compared to most other Pokémon, a few Rock-type evolutionary lines such as the Volcarona evolutionary line are fully evolved at a much later level. The type varies from strong to weak. Depending on the Rock Pokémon, they can prove to be a nuisance with stat altering moves or hard hitting attacks such as Megahorn, X-Scissor, Signal Beam and Rock Buzz. There is only one Legendary Rock-type Pokémon, Genesect.
